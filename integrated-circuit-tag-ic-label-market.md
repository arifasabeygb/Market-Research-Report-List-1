<p><p>Integrated Circuit (IC) tags, commonly known as RFID tags, are categorized into two market types: active and passive. Active IC tags contain an internal power source, allowing them to transmit information autonomously, making them ideal for long-range applications. Passive IC tags, on the other hand, derive power from the reader’s signal and are typically smaller and less expensive, suitable for short-range uses like inventory tracking. Each type serves distinct applications, balancing cost, range, and performance needs.</p></p>

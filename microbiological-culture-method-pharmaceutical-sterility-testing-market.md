<p><p>The microbiological culture method for pharmaceutical sterility testing involves two primary market types: direct inoculation and membrane filtration. Direct inoculation involves adding a sample directly into a growth medium, allowing for the detection of microorganisms by observing growth. In contrast, membrane filtration entails passing a sample through a filter that traps microorganisms, which are then transferred to a culture medium for growth observation. Both methods ensure the sterility of pharmaceutical products, catering to different testing requirements and preferences in the industry.</p></p>

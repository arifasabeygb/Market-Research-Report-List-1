Continuous kilns operate with a steady flow of materials, allowing for uninterrupted production and efficiency in high-volume operations. Conversely, batch kilns process materials in discrete loads, offering flexibility for smaller quantities and diverse products. Each type serves different production needs, influencing energy consumption, operating costs, and output capacity.</p></p>

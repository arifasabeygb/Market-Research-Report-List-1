<p><p>The semiconductor photoresist polymer market comprises several types, each with unique properties and applications. DNQ-Novolac photoresists are popular for their high-resolution imaging and thermal stability in photolithography. Epoxy-based polymers offer excellent adhesion and chemical resistance, making them suitable for diverse semiconductor processes. Off-stoichiometry thiol-ene (OSTE) polymers provide enhanced flexibility and low volatility, ideal for advanced packaging. Other types may include specialty resins designed for specific applications, catering to various fabrication needs in the semiconductor industry.</p></p>

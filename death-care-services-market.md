<p><p>The death care services market encompasses two primary types: cremation and burial. Cremation involves the reduction of a body to ashes through high-temperature combustion, often preferred for its cost-effectiveness and eco-friendliness. Additionally, cremation permits flexible memorialization options. In contrast, burial entails the interment of a body in the ground or a mausoleum, traditionally associated with religious or cultural practices. Each method reflects personal preferences, beliefs, and economic considerations, shaping individual end-of-life decisions within the market.</p></p>
